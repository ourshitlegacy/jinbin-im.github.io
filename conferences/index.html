  1. 2025 Proceedings of the International Conference on Construction Engineering and Project Management (ICCEPM) Jeju, South Korea
    A BPMN-based Framework for Real-time Rebar Arrangement Inspection on Construction Sites
    Ochirsuren. Nomgon , Gi-Tae Kim , Jin-Bin Im , Rong-Lu Hong and Ju-Hyung Kim
    Abs URL

    The efficacy of rebar arrangement inspection is limited by its labor-intensive nature as well as the challenges of conducting comprehensive surveys across large-scale construction sites. These constraints can be addressed by applying various technologies, such as artificial intelligence, image processing, drones, point clouds, and three-dimensional scanning, to reduce the opportunity cost of rebar rework through real-time inspections. However, previous studies have focused on developing technologies for specific inspection targets and have not considered their applications in the complex processes typical of the actual construction industry. This study explored the column rebar placement process to develop a real-time inspection approach that does not disrupt the critical paths. Considering the importance of process sequencing in construction, the business process modeling notation framework was employed to propose a systematic process for real-time rebar arrangement inspection. Finally, the potential benefits of this process were comprehensively outlined, suggesting its ability to improve efficiency, reduce rework, and enhance overall project performance.

  2. 2025 Proceedings of the International Conference on Construction Engineering and Project Management (ICCEPM) Jeju, South Korea
    Study of Construction Dispute Resolution Strategies Centered on Two-Level Game Theory
    Enlian Zhang , Seung-Won Choi , Jin-Bin Im , Young-Gun Baek , Ju-Hyung Kim and Kyung-Tae Lee
    Abs URL

    Conflicts between cooperative associations and contractors are common in reconstruction and redevelopment projects and often lead to project delays, cost overruns, and loss of stakeholder trust. This paper presents a conflict resolution framework grounded in two-level game theory to optimize negotiation outcomes in such projects. Through focus group interviews, this study identified key conflict drivers, including construction cost escalation, contract ambiguities, and communication breakdowns. By modeling stakeholder interactions and estimating win-set dynamics under different scenarios, this study demonstrated how internal consensus and external cooperation jointly shape negotiation success. Applied to real-world cases, this framework demonstrates the practical value of incorporating game-theoretic perspectives into construction negotiations. These findings highlight the importance of internal alignment, transparent cost verification, and third-party facilitation in resolving disputes. This study offers a structured approach to proactive conflict management and provides actionable insights for both policymakers and project managers in complex construction environments.

  3. 2025 Proceedings of the 42nd International Symposium on Automation and Robotics in Construction (ISARC) Montreal, Canada
    Effects of Visual Fidelity on Task Performance and Mental Workload in IVEs
    Jin-Bin Im , Rong-Lu Hong , Jae-Ho Hong , Enlian Zhang , Seung-Hye Shin , Moonboo Joo , Ki-Won Lee , Kang-Moo Lee , Kyung-Ho Lee , Seo-Young Park and Ju-Hyung Kim
    Abs URL

    Visual fidelity affects the user experience and performance in virtual environments. While past studies have explored its effects, most have relied on qualitative measures, and few quantitative studies have specified visual quality standards. This study examined the influence of visual fidelity on task performance and mental workload in immersive virtual environments (IVEs). Two environmental models were tested in virtual reality (VR) and mixed reality (MR): a high-visual quality model featuring detailed materials, realistic lighting, and outdoor scenery, and a low-fidelity research model with basic colors, dimensional lighting, and no external views. Sixty participants completed cognitive tasks (digit span, spatial working memory updating, go/no-go), while EEG measured mental workload. The results showed that high-fidelity increased cognitive load in complex tasks, whereas MR environments supported better performance. These findings emphasize the need to balance realism and cognitive demands to optimize virtual experiences.

  4. 2025 Proceedings of the 42nd International Symposium on Automation and Robotics in Construction (ISARC) Montreal, Canada
    Development of a Petrochemical Plant Pipe Counting Method Based on Improved Mask R-CNN and Transformer
    Rong-Lu Hong , Jin-Bin Im , Sang-Jun Park , En-Lian Zhang , Nomgon Ochirsuren , Young-Gun Beak , Shin-Hyun Kang , Seong-Won Chung , Cheol-Su Lee , Seunghyeon Wang and Ju-Hyung Kim
    Abs URL

    In petrochemical plant construction, pipe installation critically affects overall progress and costs, making accurate on-site pipe quantification essential. This study constructs the "PetroPipe" dataset, comprising 2,380 images under diverse resolutions and proposes a counting method integrating instance segmentation with a counting head and a transformer encoder. The model improves counting accuracy from 65.55% to 85.50% on the test set and achieves \~98.1% on an unlabeled 18-second real-world video, demonstrating robustness in practical conditions.

  1. 2024 Proceedings of the International Conference on Construction Engineering and Project Management (ICCEPM) Sapporo, Japan
    A Framework for Assessing the Learning Performance and Creativity in Spatial Features by Immersive Virtual Environments
    Jae-ho Jang , Jin-Bin Im , En-Lian Zhang , Moon-Boo Joo , Shin-Hyun Kang and Ju-Hyung Kim
    Abs URL

    The development of immersive virtual environment (IVE) technologies has allowed for virtual simulations and exploration of architectural spaces before building facilities. This study proposes a framework to compare learning abilities in real space and identical ones implemented via VR and MR. Various cognitive and creativity tests are conducted (N-back, Go/No-go, spatial working memory updating, Torrance Test of Creative Thinking-verbal).
